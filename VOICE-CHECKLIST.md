# VOICE CHECKLIST — Luis Ramirez Acosta
# For Claude Code: Every line of copy on this site must pass this list.

---

## ❌ NEVER DO

- **No copywriting frameworks.** No hook-problem-solution-CTA structure. No "The 3 things that will change your business." If it smells like a funnel, delete it.
- **No self-promotion verbs.** Never: "I help," "I transform," "I empower," "I specialize in," "I'm an expert in." The site doesn't say Luis is good — it shows it.
- **No urgency or fear.** No "in a world where AI is changing everything..." No "most businesses will be left behind." No manufactured stakes.
- **No filler transitions.** No "But there's more," "And here's where it gets interesting," "In other words," "To put it simply." Cut them.
- **No explaining what you just showed.** If a spec showed the point, don't restate it in the description.
- **No glazing.** No "visionary founder," "pioneer," "passionate about," "dedicated to." Just facts.
- **No selly language.** If a sentence sounds like a sales page, it's wrong. If it sounds like thinking out loud, it's right.
- **No redundancy.** If two sentences say the same thing, keep one. Delete the other.
- **No filler adjectives.** "Powerful," "robust," "cutting-edge," "innovative," "world-class" — all banned.
- **No announcing.** Don't write "Here you'll find..." or "This section covers..." Just say the thing.

---

## ✅ ALWAYS DO

- **Direct over clever.** "I work with founders who need technical leadership" beats "Your technical vision, realized."
- **Specific over general.** "12-15 deploys per week" beats "fast delivery." "Mérida, Yucatán" beats "Mexico."
- **Earn every sentence.** If a sentence doesn't add information a prospect needs, delete it.
- **One idea per section.** Don't combine two thoughts in one paragraph. If it wants to be two, make it two.
- **End clean.** The last line of any section should land with weight or end simply. Never trail off.
- **Conversational but precise.** Write like Luis would explain it over coffee with someone he respects.
- **Short sentences > long sentences.** When in doubt, cut the sentence in half.

---

## WEBSITE-SPECIFIC RULES

- **About section:** Write to the prospect. What does Luis do for THEM, not a bio of who he is.
- **Taglines under products:** One-liners only. Max 8 words. No verb + noun + adjective soup.
- **Spec lines on hardware:** Facts only. No editorial spin. "24/7 uptime" not "Never misses a beat."
- **Work cards:** Role title first. One-sentence description. No outcomes invented without proof.
- **Stack grid:** Tool name. That's it. No descriptions needed in the grid.
- **Bio line in header:** "CEO & Founder, EVEN Venture Studio. Mérida, MX." Nothing more.

---

## QUICK TEST BEFORE FINALIZING ANY COPY

1. Would Luis say this at dinner with someone he respects? → If no, rewrite.
2. Does this sound like selling? → If yes, delete.
3. Is there a copywriting pattern hiding in here? → If yes, remove it.
4. Can any sentence be cut without losing meaning? → Cut it.
5. Does the last line of each section land? → If not, rewrite the close.
